Study material

Learning goals

  • Read, write, order and compare numbers up to at least 1,000,000.
  • Round whole numbers to the nearest 10, 100, 1,000 and 10,000.
  • Use negative numbers in context and read Roman numerals.

Mini lesson

Look at each digit’s place to understand its value. For example, in 348,612 the digit 4 means 40,000. When rounding, find the rounding place, look at the digit to the right, then round up if it is 5 or more.

Worked examples

  1. 47,382 rounded to the nearest 1,000 is 47,000 because the hundreds digit is 3.
  2. In 506,219, the digit 6 has the value 6,000.
  3. If the temperature is 3°C and drops by 8°C, the new temperature is −5°C.
